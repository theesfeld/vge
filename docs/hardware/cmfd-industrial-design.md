# CMFD industrial design language

**Issue:** [#137](https://github.com/theesfeld/mfd/issues/137)

## Priority stack

1. **Lockheed CMFD layout** — square glass, 5 OSB per side, corner rockers, readable at a glance.  
2. **Late-80s Japanese consumer hardware** — soft outer radii, silver/white face, Walkman blue panels, compact brick.  
3. **Mecha / Robotech–Gundam cues** — panel lines, scuff-friendly paint, utilitarian fasteners.

## Color (first article)

| Surface | Color |
|---------|--------|
| Front outer ring | Light silver / cool grey |
| Face plate insert | **Walkman blue** (#2A6DB5 class) |
| OSB caps | Off-white / light grey |
| Rockers | Medium grey |
| Rear shell | Medium cool grey |
| Bumpers | Charcoal TPU |
| Silk / legends | White or light grey on blue; dark on silver |

## Finish

- PETG/ABS print: vapor smooth optional; paint with adhesion primer.  
- Optional **scuff/dent** is cosmetic (paint technique), not structural damage.  
- Legends: engrave + fill or printed overlays under clear coat.

## Typography on hardware

- Product wordmark: simple geometric sans (not a trademarked OEM jet logo).  
- Do **not** print “Lockheed”, “F-16”, or official NSNs on the shell.  
- Acceptable: “CMFD”, project name, port IDs, OSB numbers 1–20.
